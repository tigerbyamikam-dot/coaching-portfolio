About

A Coach Who Leads From Experience

Amikam Yalovetzky brings a rare combination of credentials to the sideline. As a retired Captain of the Israeli Air Force, he learned to lead under high-pressure, high-stakes conditions — the kind that forge real discipline and unshakeable composure. His engineering background drives a systematic, data-informed approach to player development and game preparation.

A three-time Amazon International Bestselling Author, Amikam understands how to communicate vision, motivate people, and build lasting programs. These qualities translate directly to the field — where he runs meticulously planned sessions designed around player decision-making, not just execution.

His coaching philosophy centers on one question: "Figure it out." He develops players who think for themselves, read the game, and lead without being told. The result is a team that competes — and a program that grows year over year.

Multilingual in English, Spanish, and Hebrew, Amikam connects with athletes from all backgrounds and builds the kind of inclusive team culture that keeps players coming back.

Coaching Philosophy

Develop the Person.
Build the Player.

"Real leadership comes from a deep commitment to providing a holistic environment that meets players' needs — to belong, to contribute, to do meaningful work, and to have an opportunity to grow."

01

Integrity

Be a role model. Every interaction models what it means to be a great teammate, competitor, and person. Actions speak before words.

02

Player-Centric

Understand each player as a human being before an athlete. Unconditional individual attention drives the confidence needed to take risks and grow.

03

Goals Oriented

SMART goals — process, performance, and outcome — give every player and the team direction, accountability, and measurable progress milestones.

04

Teamwork

Agreed-upon team behaviors and healthy dialogue build the trust that converts a group of players into a unit that fights for each other.

05

Safe Environment

Physical safety (ACL prevention, injury protocols) and psychological safety (inclusive culture) are non-negotiable foundations of a great program.

06

Constant Improvement

Aligned with the US Soccer Player Development Framework (Experience → Reflection → Development → Application). The coach grows alongside the players.
